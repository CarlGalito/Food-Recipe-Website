What is Adobo?

Adobo is a beloved Filipino dish made by braising meat in vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns. It has a rich, savory, and slightly tangy taste that makes it a national favorite.

Adobo Variations

Adobo has different styles depending on the region and personal preferences.

✔ Chicken Adobo

The most common variation, using chicken as the protein.

✔ Pork Adobo

Uses pork belly or shoulder for a richer, juicier flavor.

Pork Adobo Recipe

Ingredients:

  • ✔ 1 kg pork belly (cut into chunks)
  • ✔ 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • ✔ 1/2 cup vinegar
  • ✔ 5 cloves garlic (minced)
  • ✔ 1 tsp black peppercorns
  • ✔ 2 bay leaves
  • ✔ 1 cup water
  • ✔ 1 tbsp cooking oil
  • ✔ 1 tbsp sugar (optional, for a slightly sweet taste)
  • ✔ 1 tsp salt (to taste)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, marinate the pork in soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and black peppercorns for at least 1 hour.
  2. Heat oil in a pan and sauté the marinated pork until lightly browned.
  3. Pour in the reserved marinade, add bay leaves and water, then bring to a boil.
  4. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 40-50 minutes until the pork is tender.
  5. Add sugar and salt to taste, and continue simmering until the sauce thickens.
  6. For extra flavor, slightly fry the pork before serving.
  7. Serve hot with steamed rice. Enjoy your Pork Adobo!

Chicken Adobo Recipe

Ingredients:

  • ✔ 1 kg chicken (cut into serving pieces)
  • ✔ 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • ✔ 1/2 cup vinegar
  • ✔ 5 cloves garlic (minced)
  • ✔ 1 tsp black peppercorns
  • ✔ 2 bay leaves
  • ✔ 1 cup water
  • ✔ 1 tbsp cooking oil
  • ✔ 1 tbsp sugar (optional, for a slightly sweet taste)
  • ✔ 1 tsp salt (to taste)

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the chicken in soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and black peppercorns for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Heat oil in a pan and sauté the marinated chicken until golden brown.
  3. Pour in the marinade, add bay leaves and water, then bring to a boil.
  4. Lower heat and let it simmer for 30-40 minutes until the chicken is fully cooked.
  5. Add sugar and salt to taste, then continue simmering until the sauce thickens.
  6. For a crispier texture, you can fry the chicken before serving.
  7. Serve hot with steamed rice. Enjoy your Chicken Adobo!
