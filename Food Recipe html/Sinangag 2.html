What is Sinangag (Garlic Fried Rice)?

Sinangag, (Garlic Fried Rice), is a Filipino dish made by frying cooked rice with lots of garlic. Its usually served as a side dish with breakfast or any meal. The rice becomes flavorful and a little crispy from the garlic. It’s simple, tasty, and often enjoyed with eggs, sausage, or any meat!

Sinangag (Garlic Fried Rice) Variations

Sinangag (Garlic Fried Rice) can be prepared in various ways, depending on regional preferences and personal tastes.

✔ Classic Pinoy Breakfast Sinangag (Garlic Fried Rice)

The traditional version made with itlog and longganisa.

✔ Sinangag (Garlic Fried Rice) with Itlog

Scrambled or fried eggs are mixed in, adding a rich and creamy texture to the rice..

✔ Sinangag (Garlic Fried Rice) with Longganisa

(Filipino sausage) is added for a sweet and savory flavor.

Sinangag (Garlic Fried Rice) with itlog Recipe

Ingredients:

  • ✔ 2 cups cooked rice (preferably cold, day-old rice works best)
  • ✔ 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • ✔ 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • ✔ 2 eggs
  • ✔ Salt and pepper to taste
  • ✔ Optional: Chopped green onions for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Rice:
    2. If you're using leftover rice, break up any clumps so the grains are separated.
  2. Cook the Garlic:
    3. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pan or wok over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, add the minced garlic and cook it for about 1-2 minutes until it becomes fragrant and golden brown. Be careful not to burn the garlic.
  3. Scramble the Eggs:
    4. Push the garlic to the side of the pan and crack the eggs into the empty space. Scramble them with a spatula until fully cooked.
  4. Fry the Rice:
    5. Add the cooked rice to the pan and stir everything together. Make sure the rice is well-coated with the garlic and eggs. Stir-fry for about 3-5 minutes until the rice is heated through and slightly crispy.
  5. Season:
    6. Add salt and pepper to taste. Stir well to evenly season the rice.
  6. Serve:
    7. Transfer the rice to a serving dish and garnish with chopped green onions if desired. Serve it as a side dish or with your favorite main course.

Sinangag (Garlic Fried Rice) with Longganisa Recipe

Ingredients:

  • ✔ 2 cups cooked rice (preferably cold, day-old rice works best)
  • ✔ 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • ✔ 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • ✔ 2-3 pieces longganisa (Filipino sweet sausage), sliced
  • ✔ Salt and pepper to taste
  • ✔ Optional: Chopped green onions for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Rice:
    2. If you're using leftover rice, break up any clumps so the grains are separated.
  2. Cook the Longganisa:
    3. In a pan, heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the sliced longganisa and cook until browned and crispy, about 5-7 minutes. Remove the longganisa from the pan and set it aside.
  3. Cook the Garlic:
    4. In the same pan, add another tablespoon of oil and heat it. Add the minced garlic and cook for about 1-2 minutes until it becomes fragrant and golden brown.
  4. Fry the Rice:
    5. Add the cold cooked rice into the pan with the garlic and stir to combine. Cook for about 3-5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the rice is heated through and slightly crispy.
  5. Add Longganisa:
    6. Add the cooked longganisa back into the pan and stir everything together. Make sure the rice and sausage are evenly mixed.
  6. Season:
    7. Add salt and pepper to taste. Stir well to evenly season the rice.
  7. Serve:
    8. Transfer the rice to a serving dish and garnish with chopped green onions if desired. Serve as a side dish or with your favorite main course.
